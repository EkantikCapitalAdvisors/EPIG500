Risk Management Cadence

◆ Daily · Weekly · Recovery

The Fidelity Layer (OP-01..OP-04) governs whether the published method is running. The cadence below governs how aggressively the engine deploys when it is running — a dampener tier between "normal" and "cessation." Three timeframes; rules tighten as losses cluster; sizing reinstates only on observed recovery.

Daily

One day, one shot

OP-02 One-loss daily rule

After any closed losing trade, no further entries that session. The day ends on the first loss — regardless of setup quality, conviction, or remaining time.

OP-03 Trailing daily cap

Daily P&L floor anchored to a trailing high-water mark. Starts at −0.5% of NLV (one trade's worth of risk) and ratchets up as accumulated profit creates buffer. Same-day P&L cannot pierce HWM − (0.5% NLV + retained profit since prior peak). Replaces the v1.0 fixed −12 ES pts cap.

· Auto-resume

Engine resumes at standard sizing on the next trading session. No witness review required for an isolated daily-cap event.

Weekly

Two strikes, sit down

OP-05 Weekly sit-down trigger (NEW)

2–3 consecutive losing days within a single week halts trading for the remainder of that week. Engine resumes Monday with standard sizing pending witness review.

· Witness audit

During the sit-down, witness reviews the loss cluster: process breach (H2) or variance (H3)? Trade attribution log filed within 48 hours.

· Logged, not killed

A single weekly sit-down is a Tier-1 event — logged in the Discord journal, investor disclosure within 7 days. No size reduction yet.

Bi-weekly · Recovery

Two losing weeks → reduce

OP-06 Size reduction (NEW)

2nd consecutive losing week → engine sizing cuts to 1/5 of normal (e.g. 1 /MES instead of 1 /ES). Daily filing under witness oversight for the duration.

OP-07 Reinstatement (NEW)

Standard sizing reinstates only after 5 good trades at reduced size — defined as 5 closed trades with no OP breach and net positive P&L. Witness countersignature on resumption.

· Escalation

If reduced-size window produces a 3rd consecutive losing week, escalates to Stage-1 Tier-2 cessation. Stage-2 (Edge Gate) is independent and may fire at any time.

The cadence rules above operate independently of the Stage-2 Edge Gate (ED-01..ED-08). Stage-2 measures the edge across a 100-trade rolling window; the cadence above measures variance clustering at intraday/weekly horizons. Both run in parallel. Either can pause or halt the engine.

The aspirational equity curve

◆ What 12 months might look like — engine scaling on full-portfolio collateral + SPY overlay

The architectural point first. The 10% cash isn't isolated risk capital. It absorbs daily futures settlement; the SPY foundation serves as margin collateral for the engine. Position sizing is anchored at 0.5% of total NLV per trade — the engine deploys against the full portfolio, not just the 10% sleeve. So the engine's contribution is properly expressed as a % of total NLV. Target: +26% NLV from engine scaling (steps grow as position scales 1 → 5 /ES). On top, SPY foundation at the 20-year historical average (~10% × 90% allocation = +9% NLV). Combined target: ~+35% on total NLV. The hard constraint: never lose more than 5% of total NLV from any peak — the trailing floor ratchets up with every new high-water mark.

Architectural note: the 10% cash sleeve absorbs futures settlement; the 90% SPY position serves as portfolio-margin collateral, so the engine sizes against total NLV (not just the 10% cash). Position sizing is anchored at 0.5% of total NLV per trade. The chart shows the shape the discipline framework is engineered to produce — bounded drawdowns capped at −5% of total NLV from any peak, stair-step appreciation with position-sizing-up (1 → 5 /ES) as profit buffer accumulates, and SPY foundation compounding at the 20-year historical average. Headline targets (+26% engine NLV, +9% SPY NLV, +35% combined) are targets, not forecasts; live performance will look different. The trailing floor at HWM − 5% NLV ratchets up with every new high-water mark, but markets can gap and slip; nothing on this page is a guarantee.
