◆ The Architecture

What you actually own

Ekantik 500 is structured as a separately managed account (SMA) at Interactive Brokers — your account, your name, your custody. You retain ownership of every position. Ekantik holds limited trading authority bounded to futures and options activity only, executed under a Limited Power of Attorney with explicit contractual boundaries.

Inside your dedicated sub-account, three things live: the bulk of your capital sits in passive SPY exposure (your existing market participation, untouched), a small operational cash buffer absorbs daily futures settlement, and the futures overlay engine compounds disciplined risk into exponential capital on top of both.

The cash buffer is not parked capital waiting to be deployed. It is the operational shock absorber that allows the engine to run without disturbing your SPY position on adverse days. Ten percent is the architecture; ninety percent is the market exposure you already wanted.

◆ Disciplined Risk

Risk per trade stays anchored at approximately 0.5% of total NLV. At every step. The dollar amount grows because the capital base grows. The percentage never escalates.

◆ Earned Leverage

Position size increases only when verified profits compound the base. The second contract is paid for by the first. Scale is earned — never borrowed, never assumed.

◆ Velocity Compounds

Each step shortens the time to the next. By Step 5, the base is doing the heavy lifting and dollar outcomes climb non-linearly.

◆ The Falsifiability Layer

Falsifiability — The conditions under which Ekantik 500 stops.

An experiment without a falsifiability condition is not an experiment. It is faith. Ekantik 500 publishes two — both binding, both written before any capital is deployed, both observable in real time.

Two layers run in parallel from day one. Stage 1 — the Fidelity Layer watches whether the published method is what is actually running. Stage 2 — the Expression Layer watches whether the validated edge is still expressing.

Stage 1

The Fidelity Layer — four binding criteria

Stage 1 falsifies discipline, not data. A profitable rule breach is still a rule breach — and it disqualifies the trade as evidence of edge.

◆ OP-01

Frozen Process Adherence

Every entry follows the published protocol without modification. No discretionary deviation under stress. The witness — not the operator — approves any rule change, with a 48-hour cool-off, written justification, and disclosure to investors within 24 hours.

Threshold: Zero unannounced modifications, period.

Remediation: Witness countersignature required. Any breach → Tier-3 full cessation.

◆ OP-02

One-Loss Daily Rule

After any closed losing trade, no further entries that session. The day ends on the first loss — regardless of setup quality, conviction, or remaining time. This is the load-bearing intraday rule.

Threshold: Zero second-trades-after-a-loss per rolling 30 trading days.

Remediation: Per breach — trade disqualified, logged as H2. Two breaches in rolling 30 days → Tier-2 cessation.

◆ OP-03

Daily −12 Point Cap

Cumulative same-day P&L cannot reach −12 /ES points. Functions as the backstop for the single edge case where the first trade of the session is a >−12 pt loss (slipped stop, gap, structural break).

Threshold: Zero session-cap breaches per calendar quarter.

Remediation: Two breaches in any quarter → Tier-2 cessation and full process review.

◆ OP-04

Per-Trade Attribution

Every closed trade tagged within 24 hours as H2 (process breach) or H3 (variance). H1 (edge failure) is reserved for the Stage 2 rolling-100 verdict and is never applied per-trade — a single-trade H1 tag is itself a Stage-1 breach.

Threshold: Zero missing tags in rolling 100 trades.

Remediation: Per missing tag — trade disqualified. Per H1 misapplication — Tier-2 stand-down.

◆ The Binding Interpretation Rule

Stage 2 (Edge Gate) firings that occur while a Stage 1 T2 or T3 Fidelity breach is active in the rolling 30-day window are interpretation-suspended pending remediation. The edge cannot be declared falsified from data generated during a known transmission-fidelity breach. This is the load-bearing commitment that makes the two-gate architecture coherent.

◆ When ED-01 through ED-08 fire

  1. Immediate stand-down. New entries drop to zero size next session. Open positions close at their next defined exit per original trade plan.
  2. Investor disclosure within 24 hours — including the rolling-100 expectancy figure and the trade ID that satisfied the trigger.
  3. Re-deploy gate: earned re-entry only. Three requirements — (a) fresh out-of-sample test of ≥ +$50/trade over 50 qualified trades on virtual/paper/prop-firm capital, (b) written re-derivation of the structural cause of decay, (c) 48-hour cool-off. All three required.
  4. Witness role. Stage 2 trigger classification, re-entry approval, and modification approval are countersigned. The operator under loss is a different cognitive agent than the operator who set the rules.

Falsifiability gates describe operating commitments — written, witnessed, and dated before any capital is deployed. They do not constitute guarantees about future performance. Kill annotations describe operating commitments by the firm. Compliance with kill conditions does not guarantee profitable outcomes; gate-fires do not guarantee preservation of capital.

◆ FAQ

Common questions

Do I need to sell my existing portfolio?

No. Ekantik 500 is funded with new capital deposited into a dedicated sub-account at Interactive Brokers. Your existing holdings at other brokerages stay where they are.

What happens to my SPY position?

Your SPY allocation serves two purposes inside the sub-account: ongoing market exposure (dividends and capital appreciation) and margin collateral for the active sleeve. We do not trade your SPY — it sits passively while the futures overlay operates against it.

How is the 6% hurdle calculated?

Performance fees apply only after your account exceeds the high-water mark by 6% on an annualized basis. In any period where the strategy delivers less than 6%, no performance fee is charged. The hurdle resets annually to your peak.

What is the worst-case drawdown I should expect?

The methodology's backtested maximum drawdown over fifteen years was substantially less than that of pure SPY exposure. ED-05 hard-caps realized drawdown at −30 /ES points before the kill conditions force a stand-down. That said, all investing involves risk, and actual outcomes may differ from backtested results.

How would I know if the strategy has stopped working?

Ekantik 500 publishes two falsifiability gates — both binding, both observable in real time. The Fidelity Layer (four operator-discipline criteria) and the Expression Layer (eight statistical kill conditions) define exactly when the strategy stops. If any gate fires, you receive notification within 24 hours, the active engine immediately reduces to zero new exposure, and we publish the trigger condition that satisfied the gate. There is no scenario in which the strategy continues operating after a gate has fired.

Can I see the underlying trade record?

Yes. The reference dataset — 227 closed trades from April 2023 through October 2024 — is downloadable as a CSV from the Reference Dataset section. The same battery you can run on our data, you can run on yours. Once your sub-account is open, real-time broker access exposes every fill in your own account as it happens.

What is the difference between the 15-year backtest and the reference dataset?

The 15-year backtest applies the underlying methodology to S&P 500 data from 2010 through 2024 — establishing structural design intent across every major regime. The reference dataset is the operator's actual live trading record on the same methodology over 18 months, showing real-world execution against the design. Both are presented in equal weight: the backtest answers "does the architecture work in principle?"; the reference dataset answers "does the operator execute the architecture in practice?"

Can I withdraw capital at any time?

Yes. Ekantik 500 sub-accounts have no lock-up provisions. You may withdraw capital subject to ordinary settlement timelines at the custodian.

Who is Ekantik Capital Advisors LLC accountable to?

The Securities and Exchange Commission (as a Registered Investment Advisor), the IRS, our fiduciary duty to every client, and the BAPS Endowment trustees who receive 100% of our performance fees. Multi-directional accountability is by design.

How do I know my capital is safe?

Your capital is custodied at Interactive Brokers, not at Ekantik. We never touch client cash. Our limited trading authority is bounded to futures and options activity in your sub-account only. Limits are documented in the Letter of Direction, which you sign and can revoke at any time.

What if I want to change my configuration?

You may move between Standard Booster and Enhanced Booster by adjusting engine intensity (the /ES contracts per $100K NLV). The 90/10 architecture stays the same; what changes is how aggressively the engine runs.
