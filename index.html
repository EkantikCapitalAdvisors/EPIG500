◆ The Centerpiece

The Compounding Ladder

The operational mechanism of Ekantik 500 — visualized.

◆ Disciplined Risk

Risk per trade stays constant at approximately 0.5% of capital. At every step. This isn't aspiration — it's mathematics. The dollar amount grows because the capital base grows. The percentage never escalates.

◆ Earned Leverage

Position size increases only when verified profits compound the base. The second contract is paid for by the first contract's profits. The third by the second. Scale is earned — never borrowed, never assumed.

◆ Velocity Compounds

Each step shortens the time to the next. The first contract takes time to deliver compounding profits. The second contract works on a larger base, accelerating accumulation. By Step 5, the base is doing the heavy lifting and the dollar outcomes climb non-linearly.

This is not a trading product. It is a disciplined wealth architecture, expressed as a ladder, executed as a system.

◆ Architecture

What you actually own

Ekantik 500 is structured as a separately managed account (SMA) at Interactive Brokers — your account, your name, your custody. You retain ownership of every position. Ekantik holds limited trading authority over the active sleeve only, executed under a Limited Power of Attorney with explicit contractual boundaries.

Inside your dedicated sub-account, two things live: a passive equity foundation (typically SPY) that provides market exposure and serves as margin collateral, and an active cash sleeve where the disciplined futures overlay operates.

The two work together. The equity foundation compounds at the market's pace. The active sleeve climbs The Compounding Ladder. The integrated result is exponentially superior to either component alone.

  1. Your sub-account, your custody

    Opened at Interactive Brokers in your name. You hold beneficial ownership of every dollar.

  2. Limited trading authority

    Granted via a one-page Letter of Direction. Authority is strictly bounded to futures and options activity. Revocable at any time.

  3. Mixed-collateral funding

    Your account holds passive SPY exposure as margin collateral plus active cash for the overlay strategy. You choose the mix.

  4. Real-time transparency

    Read-only broker access shows every trade, every position, every dollar — in real time, anytime.

  5. Performance fee anchored to results

    6% high-watermark hurdle plus 25% of profits above that hurdle. We earn when you earn — and only when you earn.

  6. Philanthropic alignment

    100% of performance fees flow to the BAPS Endowment. Ekantik takes zero personal equity from the firm.

◆ FAQ

Common questions

Do I need to sell my existing portfolio?

No. Ekantik 500 is funded with new capital deposited into a dedicated sub-account at Interactive Brokers. Your existing holdings at other brokerages stay where they are.

What happens to my SPY position?

Within the sub-account, your SPY allocation serves two purposes: it provides ongoing market exposure (dividends and capital appreciation) and acts as margin collateral for the active sleeve. We do not trade your SPY — it sits passively while the futures overlay operates against it.

How is the 6% hurdle calculated?

Performance fees apply only after your account exceeds the high-water mark by 6% on an annualized basis. In any period where the strategy delivers less than 6%, no performance fee is charged. The hurdle resets annually to your peak.

What is the worst-case drawdown I should expect?

The methodology's backtested maximum drawdown over fifteen years was substantially less than that of pure SPY exposure. That said, all investing involves risk, and actual outcomes may differ from backtested results. We disclose maximum drawdown expectations as part of your onboarding consultation, calibrated to your specific configuration choice.

Can I withdraw capital at any time?

Yes. Ekantik 500 sub-accounts have no lock-up provisions. You may withdraw capital subject to ordinary settlement timelines at the custodian.

Who is Ekantik Capital Advisors LLC accountable to?

The Securities and Exchange Commission (as a Registered Investment Advisor), the IRS, our fiduciary duty to every client, and the BAPS Endowment trustees who receive 100% of our performance fees. Multi-directional accountability is by design.

How do I know my capital is safe?

Your capital is custodied at Interactive Brokers, not at Ekantik. We never touch client cash. Our limited trading authority is bounded to futures and options activity in your sub-account only. Limits are documented in the Letter of Direction, which you sign and can revoke at any time.

What if I want to change my configuration?

You may move between Standard and Enhanced configurations by reallocating cash between the equity foundation and the active sleeve. This is an internal account adjustment requiring no new paperwork.

Is there a minimum holding period?

No formal minimum, but the strategy is designed for multi-year compounding. The Compounding Ladder builds value over quarters and years; entry-and-exit timing tends to undermine the underlying mechanism.

Who do I talk to before deciding?
